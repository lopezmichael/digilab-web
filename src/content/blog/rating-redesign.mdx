# Rating System v2.0

We've redesigned how DigiLab calculates player ratings. This post explains what changed, why we made these changes, and what it means for you.

## The Problem with the Old System

The previous rating system had several issues:

1. **5-pass iteration** — Recalculated everything when any tournament was added
2. **Time-based decay** — Recent tournaments counted more, anchored to "today"
3. **Ties as losses** — Tied placements were treated as 0 instead of 0.5

This created a flat rating distribution that didn't accurately reflect skill differences.

## What Changed

The new algorithm:

1. **Single-pass chronological** — Processes tournaments once in date order
2. **No decay** — Standard Elo doesn't use time-based weighting
3. **Proper tie handling** — Ties are 0.5 (draw)

## How It Affects You

- **Rankings matter more than raw numbers** — Positions are more stable than the numbers suggest
- **The spread was artificial** — Decay made it look like there was more skill difference than existed
- **New system is fairer** — Players aren't punished for taking breaks
- **Everyone's numbers compressed** — It's not that anyone got worse, the scale is more accurate
